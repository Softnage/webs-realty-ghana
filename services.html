To purchase a property in Dubai, you typically need:
  • A valid passport.
  • A visa or Emirates ID
  • A copy of your trade license
  • Proof of income or a pre-approval letter if you're using a mortgage.

    The Dubai Land Department (DLD) charges a transfer fee of 4% of the property’s sale value, plus an administrative fee of AED 580 for apartments and AED 430 for land or villas.

  • Step 1: Choose a property and agree on the price with the seller.
  • Step 2: Sign a Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) and pay the deposit (usually 10%).
  • Step 3: Apply for a No Objection Certificate (NOC) from the developer.
  • Step 4: Complete the transfer process at the Dubai Land Department.
  • Step 5: Pay the transfer fee, registration fee, and any outstanding service charges.
  • Step 6: Receive the title deed to officially own the property.
