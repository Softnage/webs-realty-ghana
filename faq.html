Properties in Dubai are registered through the Dubai Land Department (DLD), which is the legally authorized entity for documenting all real estate transactions. Registration is conducted via DLD’s electronic system (e-register) or through Real Estate Registration Trustee centers. Developers use a self-registration system for properties they sell, while individual transactions must be completed by owners or their representatives with an official power of attorney.

Dubai offers two types of real estate residency visas:

  • ➤Three-Year Visa: Available for properties valued at AED 750,000 or more.
  • ➤Five-Year Visa: Granted for properties exceeding AED 5 million in value.

Yes, all real estate transactions, including ownership transfers, must be registered with the Dubai Land Department to guarantee the rights of investors. Unregistered transactions are considered invalid under Dubai’s real estate laws.

Initial registration involves recording real estate sales contracts and other legal actions before transferring them to the Real Estate Registry. This applies to both off-plan and ready-made properties, ensuring the rights of owners and investors are preserved.

Once a property is fully paid for and the developer receives a completion certificate from relevant authorities, the property is transferred from the initial registry to the Real Estate Registry, and the title deed is issued to the owner.

  • Increase in Area: Any excess in the net area is not considered unless otherwise agreed in the sales contract.
  • Decrease in Area: If the property area is reduced by more than 5% of the net area stated in the sales contract, the developer is obligated to compensate the buyer for the difference.

    There is no age limit for property ownership in Dubai. However:

  • For minors under 21 years old, their legal guardian must sign any sales contracts or transactions on their behalf.
  • Selling, mortgaging, or donating property owned by a minor requires approval from the Awqaf and Minors' Affairs Foundation (for UAE citizens) or the competent court (for other categories).

    • Properties eligible for mortgages must meet the following conditions:

  • The property must be registered with the Dubai Land Department.
  • The property must be completed and insurable at the time of the mortgage.
  • Mortgage agreements must comply with Dubai’s laws and regulations.
